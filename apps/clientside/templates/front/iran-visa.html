So you’re thinking about visiting Iran

Before You Go

  1. Get a private tour with a local.Customizing your own experience with small private tours are the cheapest, most flexible alternative to group tours. TAP Persia offers over 100 different half-day, full-day & multi-day Iran tours to create the ultimate tailor-made experience.
  2. Group tours are extremely common.Group tours generally start and end in Tehran, with stops in the must-see cities of Shiraz, Esfahan, and Yazd along with several minor diversions.
    TAP Persia offers you the chance to pick and choose your own, custom-made Iran tour package.
    The price of the package depends on how long you intend to stay, mode of intercity transport, type of accommodations, number of participants, and the exchange rate.
  3. Safe for solo travelers, even women. It is easier for women to travel as a couple, but both sexes will have no problem traveling alone. Read Anna Karsten’s articleIs it safe to travel to Iran as a solo female traveler?(+) on the Tehran Times.
  4. Bring USD or EUR in cash. Except for a few high-end handicraft shops in tourist centers of major cities, you will not be able to use your credit or debit cards. You can find reputable exchanges by asking the reception of your accommodation.
    You can also apply for an Iran tourist card, a temporary Iranian debit card accepted at almost all shops.
    You can charge it with cash or bank transfer from any city in Iran, your funds will be exchanged and deposited in your account within 1 hour of transfer, and you can use it to withdraw cash from almost any ATM.
  5. Locals will invite you to dine with them.One of the most appealing parts of Iranian culture is inviting guests to join you for a home-cooked meal indoors or out on a picnic.
    The country is famous for its Iranian hospitality and you shouldn’t pass up an opportunity to dine with a local family.

Once you arrive

  1. Wear Islamic covering before customs. Wear pants and long sleeves. Women cover hair. Assuming you have your Iran visa authorization code, most customs officials are efficient and tourists rarely get too much hassle. Land borders can take longer if you’re on a bus or train.
  2. You’ll need an Iranian SIM card. Fast, reliable internet can be found all over the country, but you won’t be able to use your own SIM card while traveling. You can purchase your own Iran SIM card at the airport.
  3. Credit/Debit card won’t work. You will need to bring enough cash in USD or EUR for your trip. Other currencies can also be exchanged, albeit less frequently.
    If you want to avoid the hassle of carrying loads of cash, apply for an Iran tourist card. You can charge it with a cash or bank transfer from any city in Iran, your funds will be exchanged and deposited in your account within 1 hour of transfer, and you can use it to withdraw cash from almost any ATM.
  4. Flights arrive in the middle of the night.Most flights landing at IKIA or other international airports often arrive at inconvenient hours. To ensure a speedy arrival to your hotel, we recommend an Iran pick-up tour.
    A driver will be waiting for you upon your arrival, even through delays, and will take you from the airport straight to your final destination.
  5. Intercity transport is frequent & relaxing. Options such as air, rail & bus are efficient, cheap and can be bought effortlessly. You can even book your own Iran transport tickets.
  6. Attraction entrance fees are cheap. While rates are much cheaper for locals, Iran attraction entrance fees are usually 2,000,000 IRR which is around 8 USD or 6.5 EUR (as of Aug 2021) .
  7. Tourist hubs, hotels & Gen Z speak English.Inside cities, most people speak enough English and are willing to help so that you’ll never really feel lost.
    Locals consider guests in their country as “God’s Gift” and will go out of their way to make sure you are ok.

Other Iran visa types

  1. Transit Visa (Transport). Doesn’t require authorization from Tehran but is issued for 5-7 days. No Iran visa authorization code required, processed in 2-3 days.
    Mostly used by people who enter the country through a land border, those with their own cars, or as a last resort. Must enter and exit via different countries.
    Must provide a ticket to an onward country. Not available to US, UK, or Canadian passport holders.
    If you plan on road-tripping and exploring the country, apply for Iran visa before you go.
  2. Business Visa (Entry). Available for45-day (extendable). Must obtain an invitation letter from the company or organization you plan to visit. Normally for people coming for a conference or to play in a sporting event.
  3. Pilgrimage Visa (Ziarat). For Muslims who wish to visit Iranian holy sites. Religious pilgrimages to non-Islamic sites should get a regular tourist visa.
  4. Press Visa (Media).For members of the televised, digital, or audio-based press, filmographers, directors, and producers coming on official assignment.

  4. Dual citizens. If you are a dual citizen of the US, UK, or Canada, use your other passport when filling your application. You can also apply with the nationality that has a cheaper fee. Check the “Visa fee calculator”.

  6. Apply early for the high season. Most individuals can and do apply for the visa-on-arrival upon their arrival. This option does carry its own risks, wait times, and hassles. The application process will stall during the Persian New Year (Nowruz) starting from mid-March to mid-April.
  7. Entering Iran with no visa. Everyone, including citizens of the US, UK, and Canada, can visit Kish Island without a visa for 14 days. The island, located off the mainland in the Persian Gulf, is a popular travel destination for Iranians.
  8. Visiting Israel will get you denied. Israeli passport holders and anyone with an Israeli stamp in their passport (or exit stamps at relevant Jordanian or Egyptian border crossings that imply that you must have entered Israel) will not get a visa.

Customs regulations

  1. What you can bring in. Most Iranian officials are relaxed about what foreigners bring into and out of the country. Your bags will most likely not get searched at the airport.
    You are allowed to import 200 cigarettes, 50 cigars, and a “reasonable quantity” of perfume duty-free. Alcohol, pork, and porn are strictly illegal and should not be brought in.
    You can bring in pretty much any book as long as the cover doesn’t show too much female skin or hair.
    You can bring in your laptop, smartphone, shortwave radio, iPad, or video equipment if they don’t look too professional. Technically you have to declare if you have more than 1,000 USD in cash, but few do, and the officials rarely check.
  2. Drones laws are complicated. Most smaller drones are allowed, but authorities have been known to scrutinize drones and other film-making equipment. Contact us by starting a chat or sending us a message on WhatsApp for up-to-date information.
  3. What can you take home? You can bring out anything that you originally brought in, along with any other handicrafts except rugs.
    You can bring a “reasonable number” of rugs with you as long as the total value doesn’t exceed 160 USD. Save your receipts as proof of purchase. Shopkeepers will undervalue the items on receipts issued to foreigners so that they will be able to export more carpets.
    You can take up to 150 g of gold and 3 Kg of silver, without any precious gemstones. Anything over that and you have to get an export permit from the Iranian Customs Office.
    US sanctions mean that you technically can’t take more than 100 USD worth of Iranian goods into the US.

Iran Visa duration & extensions

  1. Most visas are issued for 45 days. Your Iran Tourist Visa is good for 45 days, with a chance to extend the visa an additional 60 days. There is very little difficulty in extending your visa for the first additional 45 days. However, you will have to go to a new city if you wish to extend your visa a second time, for another additional 45 days.
  2. Choose your extension location wisely. Plan your trip around a tourist-friendly city that will easily accept your extension application. Shiraz is the best choice, followed by Isfahan.  Kerman, Yazd, and Tabriz don’t always issue the full 45-day extension. Tehran, Mashhad, and other cities are not that reliable.
  3. The extension process. Locate the Passport Immigration Police or the Police Department of Aliens Affairs (edareh gozarnameh) in the city you want to extend. You will need 2-3 passport photos, 2 copies of identification pages of your passport, your current Iran Tourist Visa, your entry stamp/paperwork, any previous extensions, 2 copies of your visa extension form, and at least 1,000,000 IRR for the extension fee along with any additional fees for forms/printing/etc. (Photocopiers can usually be found in each location).
    Go to the nearest Melli Bank Melli branch (usually on-site or nearby), tell the bank staff visa and they will fill the forms for you. Pay the fee, return to the office with your bank receipt and visa extension.
    Your visa extension should be issued within 1-3 hours, however, in the case of less reliable cities, the process may take up to 3-7 days.
  4. When to extend. You should apply for your extension at least 2-3 days before the visa expires. Your extension starts on the day the extension is issued, not from the end of the original visa. Check with the Online Persian calendar to check the date. 
  5. Do not overstay. Overstaying your visa, even by a few hours, could cause you to be detained. In true emergencies, an official doctor’s note could act as a weak form of extension at the border.
